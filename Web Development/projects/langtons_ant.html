Langton's Ant Simulation

An interactive cellular automaton simulation (Work in Progress)

Langton's Ant is a cellular automaton devised by Chris Langton. In this simulation, an "ant" moves on a grid according to a simple rule (for example, on a white cell, turn right, flip the cell's color, and move forward; on a black cell, turn left, flip the cell's color, and move forward). Over time, the ant's trail produces emergent, chaotic patterns.

5 fps
Generation: 0
Alive Cells: 0

How to Play

  • Click "Start" to begin the simulation.
  • Use "Stop" to pause, "Clear" to reset the grid, and "Random" to randomize the grid.
  • Adjust the speed slider to control the simulation speed.

Rules

  • On a white cell, the ant turns right, flips the cell's color (to black), and moves forward.
  • On a black cell, the ant turns left, flips the cell's color (to white), and moves forward.
